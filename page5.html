WHAT ARE TRIGONOMETRIC RATIOS OF COMPLEMENTARY ANGLES ?

Complementary angles are those angles whose sum is 90°.
For example, in right triangle ABC right-angles at B, A and C are complementary angles.
Since ∠A+∠B+∠C= 180°
∠A+90°+∠C=180°
Therefore, ∠A+∠C=180°-90°
∠A+∠C=90°

Complementary angles would give rise to following transformation :

  • sin (90°-θ) = cos θ
  • cos (90°-θ) = sin θ
  • tan (90°-θ) = cot θ
  • cot (90°-θ) = tan θ
  • sec (90°-θ) = cosec θ
  • cosec (90°-θ) = sec θ

Let us consider some examples to make understanding of fundamental of the concept very easy and clear.

1) The value of cos 59°/sin 31°
(a) 0 (b) 2 (c) 1 (d) 3

SOLUTION:
= cos 59°/sin 31°
= cos(90°-31°)
= sin 31°/sin 31°
= 1
Therefore, option (c) is correct.

2) Evaluate :
(i) sin 18°/cos 72°
(ii) tan 26°/cot 64°
(iii) cos 48° - sin 42°
(iv) cosec 31° - sec 59°
(v) (sin 47°/cos 43°)2 + (cos 43°/ sin 47°)2 - 4 cos2 45°

SOLUTION:
(i) sin 18°/cos 72°
= sin (90°-72°)/cos 72°
= cos 72°/cos 72°
= 1

(ii) tan 26°/cot 64°
= tan(90°-64°)/cot 64°
= cot 64°/cot 64°
= 1

(iii) cos 48° - sin 42°
= cos (90°-42°) - sin 42°
= sin 42°- sin 42°
= 0

(iv) cosec 31° - sec 59°
= cosec (90°-59°) - sec 59°
= sec 59° - sec 59°
= 0

(v) (sin 47°/cos 43°)2 + (cos 43°/ sin 47°)2 - 4 cos2 45°
= {sin(90°-43°)/cos 43°}2 + {cos 43°/sin(90°-43°)}2 - 4(1/√2)2
= (cos 43°/cos 43°)2 + (cos 43°/cos 43°)2 - 4 (1/2)
= 1 + 1 - 2
= 0
